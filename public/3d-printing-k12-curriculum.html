3D Design and Fabrication (3D Printing)
K–12 Curriculum

A complete K–12 curriculum for 3D Design and Fabrication, organized across four skill stages: Maker (K–3), Designer (4–6), Engineer (7–9), and Innovator (10–12). Each grade includes 10 core topics with 40 lessons per year. Includes an optional Foundation Bootcamp for schools joining mid-program.

🎯 Learning Objectives

✅Develop spatial thinking and 3D visualization skills from an early age
✅Progress through four structured skill stages: Maker, Designer, Engineer, and Innovator
✅Learn to design, prototype, and fabricate objects using 3D printing technology
✅Apply design thinking and engineering processes to real-world problems
✅Build confidence in digital fabrication through hands-on project-based learning

🛠 Materials Required

3D printer access CAD software (e.g., Tinkercad, Fusion 360) Computers or tablets Modeling clay (early grades) Basic building materials for prototyping
